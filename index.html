The Context

The "Clustered Waste" Crisis

Khulna City generates 600 tons of solid waste daily, yet municipal capacity only covers 400 tons. The remaining 200 tons accumulate in marginalized areas, causing environmental degradation.

Currently, 98% of households are aware of the issue, but lack the infrastructure to practice source separation effectively.

Our Methodology

Source Separation Model

We replace the "dump and forget" culture with a 6-step circular economy framework:

  • Educate: Community campaigns on segregation value.
  • Infrastructure: Providing double-compartment bins.
  • Collection: Trained collectors for segregated pickup.
  • Monetization: Selling organic/inorganic materials to partners.

Operational Framework

1

Household Segregation

Households separate waste into Organic (Green) and Inorganic (Red) bins.

2

Tech-Enabled Collection

Collectors use the ReCyTage App to track pickup and verify segregation quality.

3

Resource Recovery

Organic waste goes to Green-Agro (Compost); Plastics go to recyclers (Aladin Plastic).

Sustainable Revenue Model

Subscription Fees

Monthly waste collection fee from households.

Material Sales

Selling raw materials (plastic, compost) to industrial partners.

B2B Partnerships

Collaborations with bin manufacturers and advertisers.
